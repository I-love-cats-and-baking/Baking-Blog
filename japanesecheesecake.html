Japanese Cheescake


    Servings : 1


    Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 4 ounces of cream cheese
  • 3 Tbs of butter
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 3 Tbs cornstarch
  • 6 egg whites
  • 1/3 cup white or cane sugar
  • Powdered sugar, optional

    Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 315°F
  2. In a small saucepan stirring over medium heat mix together milk, cream cheese, and butter.
  3. Strain mixture to get out any unmelted clumps and put aside.
  4. In a large mixing bowl gently whisk egg yolks just until smooth.
  5. Slowly whisk in cream mixture, you do not want to cook the egg yolk.
  6. Sift together the flour and cornstarch, then whisk into the cream mixture. Gently whisk as to not overmix
  7. In a medium mixing bowl beat the egg whites with an electric mixer until soft peaks form. Then gradually pour in the sugar while mixing.
  8. The egg whites should now form glossy but stiff peaks. Take about 1/3 of the mixture and fold it in. You can fold the first third quite vigorously, but you should be gentle when folding in the rest.
  9. You can now use either a 4.5 inch round or a springform pan lined both on the bottom and sides with parchment for baking. If you are using a springform pan, wrap the bottom in a minimum of two layers of foil to avoid leakage.
  10. Once the batter is in the pan lightly tap the pan to release any excess air bubbles
  11. Place a baking pan on the lowest rack of your oven, place your cake pan into the pan and fill the baking pan with approximately two inches of water.
  12. Bake your cake for 20 minutes, then lower the heat to 275°F and let bake for 55 minutes. After 55 minutes, turn off the heat and leave your cake to cool in the oven for 10 minutes to avoid shrinkage.
  13. While your cake is still warm transfer it to a plate. Once the cake has cooled you can sift powdered sugar on the top for a more refined look
  14. Enjoy!
