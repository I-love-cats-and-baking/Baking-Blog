Earl Grey Macarons


    Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup almond flour
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 Tbs of earl grey tealeaves
  • 2 egg whites
  • 1/4 cup caster sugar
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract

    Directions

  1. Put the almond flour and powdered sugar in a food processor and pulse 3 times. You cannot pulse more than 3 times or else the almond oil will cause the powdered sugar to clump.
  2. In a medium mixing bowl beat the egg whites with an electric mixer until soft peaks form. Then gradually pour in the sugar while mixing.
  3. Add the vanilla and continue to beat
  4. The egg whites should now form glossy but stiff peaks. Take about 1/3 of the mixture and fold it in. You can fold the first third quite vigorously, but you should be gentle when folding in the rest.
  5. You can now add coloring if you would like to.
  6. When the batter is ready it should ribbon off of your spatula. Try to avoid overfolding.
  7. Using parchment paper, pipe out your macarons onto a template, 3/4-inch round circles, approximately 1 inch apart
  8. Tap the jellyroll pan to release any air bubbles, you may need to use a toothpick to poke any unpopped bubbles.
  9. Leave out the macarons to let a thin film develop over the top of the macarons, this helps the macaron develop its feet.
  10. Preheat the oven to 325°F
  11. Bake the macarons for 5 minutes, then rotate the pan 180° and bake for an additional 5 minutes.
  12. You want to transfer the macarons to a plate while they are still slightly warm.
  13. Fill your macarons with lemon curd or lemon buttercream to get the best results.
